Dyspraxia

Dyspraxia is recognized to be a lack of development in movement control.

The effects of dyspraxia are caused by a messages not being fully transmitted by the brain. This then affects one's ability to plan for the future, percieve new information, learn language, and cognitive thinking. There cause of dyspraxia is unknown, though some research suggests it may be related to neuron development. Unfortunetely, there is no known cure for dyspraxia, but therepies can assist with overcoming the problems of dyspraxia.

Symptoms

The term of dyspraxia is used to represent a specific set of symptoms.

Dyspraxia results in some movement impairments, such as difficulties in running, jumping, and balancing. The ability to learn fine motor skills and conceptual skills can also be negatively impacted. These often result in a lack of confidence in learning new skills. "Routine tasks", like daily chores and errands, may also be more difficult for those with dyspraxia.
